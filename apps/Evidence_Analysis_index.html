How the Local AI Works

1. Parsing

Separates messages, speakers, sentences, questions, emojis, and probable quoted blocks.

2. Evidence Extraction

Finds behavioral, protective, editorial, specificity, urgency, money, secrecy, and verification signals.

3. Claim Memory

Extracts first-person claims and stores them for later comparison and human review.

4. Adaptive Feedback

User feedback adjusts local category weights. It does not train a remote model.

5. Monte Carlo Uncertainty

Produces reproducible uncertainty ranges around evidence-based scores using a content-seeded simulation.

Interpretation Guardrails

A high signal score is not proof of plagiarism, fraud, dishonesty, criminal conduct, authorship, or factual falsity. It indicates that the material deserves closer review.
  • Common phrases should not be treated as plagiarism.
  • Writing-style shifts can arise from editing, translation, disability, collaboration, stress, or AI assistance.
  • Inconsistencies may reflect context, memory, changing circumstances, or transcription errors.
  • Public-speech claims require authoritative source verification.
  • Investigative use must respect due process, privacy, and lawful evidence handling.
