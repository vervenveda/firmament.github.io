Before you talk to a lawyer

  1. Write a timeline. Note dates, what happened, who was involved, and how it affected you.
  2. Gather papers. Collect contracts, emails, screenshots, letters, receipts, photos, or messages.
  3. Write your questions. What worries you most? What outcome would feel safe or fair?

You can use the Custom Notes space inside Firmament Law to write your timeline and questions, then bring that draft to your attorney.

Short answers (FAQ)

Is Firmament Law a law firm?

No. Firmament Law is a drafting and organizing tool. It helps you gather details and create clear outlines so a licensed attorney can advise you. It does not give legal advice, represent you, or create an attorney–client relationship.

Where are my documents stored?

Your drafts are stored using your browser’s local storage on the device you are using. They are not uploaded to our servers. If you clear browser data, change browsers, or use another device, those drafts will not follow you.

Can I use these drafts without a lawyer?

We strongly recommend no. Laws are different and complex in every city, state, and country. Drafts from Firmament Law are educational starting points only and must be reviewed, customized, and approved by a qualified lawyer in your jurisdiction.

Who is Firmament Law designed for?

Firmament Law is designed for artists, families, students, educators, and founders using the Verve N Veda / PLERA ecosystem who want a calm, private place to think through legal matters before speaking with counsel.
