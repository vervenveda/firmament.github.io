Template Library

High-level overviews of each template available in the Generator so members know what they’re drafting.

  • Living Will – A personal directive about healthcare, life-support, and end-of-life wishes if you cannot speak for yourself.
  • Trust Planning Worksheet – A structured outline to organize assets, trustees, and beneficiaries for a future trust instrument.
  • Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) – A confidentiality structure for sharing sensitive information with collaborators, students, vendors, etc.

About & Safety

How Firmament Law works, what it can do for you, and the limits that keep everyone safe.

What Firmament Law Is

Firmament Law is a sovereign, local-only drafting console for PLERA, Pearls Gamut and Archaemenes Academy members. It gives you a structured place to think through legal documents, collect details, and prepare questions for your lawyer.

What Firmament Law Is Not

  • It is not a law firm or a substitute for a licensed attorney.
  • It does not give legal advice or create legally binding instruments.
  • It does not send your documents to any server or third party.

Your Local Sovereign Cloud

All drafts are stored using your browser’s localStorage under the key firmamentCloudV1. If you clear browser data, switch browsers, or use another device, those drafts will not follow you. For anything important, download and back up your documents separately in your own encrypted storage.

This button permanently deletes all Firmament Law drafts in this browser. It cannot be undone. Use it when you want a clean slate or before sharing your device.

Legalization & Status Hub

Starting points for apostilles, document authentication, and record status checks.

Use this hub to learn how to legalize documents (apostilles, authentications) and understand status requirements when you are moving documents across borders. Always confirm requirements with the official authority in your country or region.

USA – Apostilles & Authentication

Official guidance on how to get a U.S. document certified for use abroad.

  • USA.gov – Authenticate a U.S. Document
  • U.S. Dept. of State – Office of Authentications

Hague Apostille Convention

Information on the Apostille Convention and member countries.

  • HCCH – Apostille Section

State & Local Apostille Offices

Each U.S. state usually has its own apostille / authentication office (often the Secretary of State).

Search: “[Your State] Secretary of State apostille” for the official website and instructions.

Document legalization can be technical and time-sensitive. For high-stakes matters (marriage, adoption, business filings), consider asking a local attorney or notary specialist to review everything before you submit.
