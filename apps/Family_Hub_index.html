First 3 Calls

  • 211: local help with food, rent, utilities, child care, and referrals.
  • School district: meals, transportation, McKinney-Vento help, IEP/504 supports.
  • Health coverage office: Medicaid, CHIP, WIC, local clinic, and pregnancy support.

Parent Call Script

Use this when calling 211, a school, a clinic, a housing office, or a benefits agency.

Hello, my name is ________. I am a parent/caregiver in ZIP code ________. My family needs help with ________. We have children ages ________. Our most urgent need today is ________. Can you tell me: 1. What programs we may qualify for? 2. What documents we need? 3. Whether there is a waitlist or emergency option? 4. Who else should we call today? 5. Can you text or email the instructions to me? Thank you. Can I please write down your name, direct phone number, and the next step?

Parent Roadmap

1. Stabilize the urgent issue.

Use 911 for immediate danger, 988 for mental health crisis, 211 for local services, and specialized hotlines for domestic violence, trafficking, runaway youth, child abuse, or missing children.

2. Secure food, health care, and safe shelter.

Apply for SNAP/WIC/school meals, locate a food bank, call a local health center, check Medicaid/CHIP, and ask about emergency rental, shelter, or utility help.

3. Keep children connected to school and care.

Ask the school about transportation, meals, McKinney-Vento supports for housing instability, IEP/504 evaluations, after-school programs, and attendance help.

4. Build a longer-term support plan.

Use the saved plan, benefits finder, child care subsidy information, job training, legal aid, child support services, and parent centers to move from crisis support toward stability.
